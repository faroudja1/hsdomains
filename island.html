Islands are places of longing and retreat. They are a synonym of living space, freedom, independence and privacy. Islands are either idyllic like the islands of the Caribbean or the South Sea, far away from the nearest land like the island Tristan da Cunha - the most remote settlement in the world or they are very densely populated like Santa Cruz del Islote or most dangerous for us like the island North Sentinel - home to indigenous people with no contact to the outside world. They are new or very old, romantic or completely inhospitable.

More than 500 million people live on islands. Sweden itself has more than 220000 islands. Madgascar is the oldest island in the world, the island Hunga Tonga-Hunga Ha'apai is probably the youngest island in the world.

be one of the first

The top level domain ".island" belongs to the new internet - the handshake blockchain. Your domain is therefore part of a decentralised internet, free from the restrictions of the traditional ICANN domain system and still 100% compatible with the global DNS standard. Handshake is to domain names what Bitcoin is to money.

With the handshake blockchain the internet will be more secure, resilient, and socially useful with a peer-to-peer system validated by the network's participants.

Be one of the first to take advantage on the blockchain web by claiming your dream domain on .island and simply redirecting it to your existing website.

how to access your .island domain?

Handshake domains require DNS providers that are equipped to access handshake domains. NextDNS is the recommended choice for accessing domains on the handshake blockchain, it also comes with a number of useful security features to keep you safe while you browse online.

HandyBrowser is the new browser that runs on top of the Handshake protocol. It’s a dedicated way to access the uncensorable internet ecosystem made possible through the distributed Handshake Network, as well as anything else you would normally search for.

The open source browser brave has also expressed interest in Handshake and will likely be the first browser to adopt Handshake.
